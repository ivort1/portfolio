How familiar are you with DevOps and Agile/Scrum development methodologies and processes? Our team has successfully implemented a pipeline that covers development, staging, and production stages. This pipeline leverages a combination of Docker, Helm charts, Artifactory, and GitHub actions. While I wasn't involved in setting it up initially, I have gained valuable experience working with this pipeline. I have actively participated in modifying Helm charts and have successfully resolved deployment issues within GitHub actions when they arose.
To effectively manage our tasks and projects, we utilize Jira as our ticket management system. During our daily standup meetings, we review the Kanban board together, discussing individual assigned tickets and addressing any potential blockers. Our work is organized into sprints, each lasting two weeks. At the end of each sprint, we conduct a retrospective meeting to reflect on what went well and identify areas for improvement.
Prior to the start of each sprint, we hold a sprint planning meeting where we collaborate to create new tickets, assign tasks from the backlog, and establish our overall goals for the upcoming two weeks. For seamless communication and updates, all relevant information and comments are recorded within the corresponding tickets. One of our product specialists implemented a helpful integration where creating a new branch in GitHub using a ticket number automatically generates a link within the corresponding Jira ticket, which helps tremendously with tracking progress and collaboration.
And finally, we maintain a dedicated Slack channel with automated notifications enabled. This allows us to receive notifications whenever a build is released or if any failures occur, ensuring efficient communication and swift response to any issues that may arise during the development process.
